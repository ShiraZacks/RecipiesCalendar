<b>Hamentashen</b><br/><br/>
<i>These traditional Purim treats are triangular cookies filled with something sweet. Add your favorite filling!</i><br/><br/>

Ingredients:<br/>
3 eggs<br/>
1 cup granulated sugar<br/>
¾ cup vegetable oil<br/>
2 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract<br/>
½ cup orange juice<br/>
5 ½ cups all-purpose flour<br/>
1 tablespoon baking powder<br/>
Jelly, chocolate chips, or cherry pie filling<br/><br/>

Instructions:<br/>
1) Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease cookie sheets.<br/>
2)In a large bowl, beat the eggs and sugar until lightly and fluffy.<br/>
3) Stir in the oil, vanilla and orange juice.<br/>
4) Combine the flour and baking powder; stir into the batter to form a stiff dough. If dough is not stiff enough to roll out, stir in more flour.<br/>
5) On a lightly floured surface, roll dough out to 1/4 inch in thickness.<br/>
6) Cut into circles using a cookie cutter or the rim or a drinking glass.<br/>
7) Place cookies 2 inches apart onto the prepared cookie sheets.<br/>
8) Spoon some filling into the center of each one. Pinch the edges to form three corners.<br/>
9) Bake for 12 to 15 minutes in the preheated oven, or until lightly browned.<br/>
10) Allow hamentashen to cool for 1 minute on the cookie sheet before removing to wire racks to cool completely.<br/>
